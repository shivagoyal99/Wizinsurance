Why International Students Need Insurance

International students in Canada are not automatically covered by provincial health insurance (OHIP in Ontario). Most schools and study permit applications require proof of valid health coverage. Without it, a single visit to a walk-in clinic can cost hundreds of dollars.

  • Required by most Canadian universities and colleges
  • Needed for study permit applications
  • Emergency medical & hospitalization
  • Mental health support (select plans)
  • Prescription drug coverage
  • Vision and dental on enhanced plans

📚 Plans Starting from ~$600/year

Coverage for a full academic year is more affordable than most students expect. I compare GMS, Blue Cross, Manulife, and other carriers to find the most comprehensive plan at the lowest cost.
