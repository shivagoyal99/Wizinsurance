Common Questions

Does getting a new credit card hurt my credit score?
A hard inquiry typically drops your score by 5–10 points temporarily. With responsible use, your score usually recovers within 3–6 months and often improves long-term through higher available credit and positive payment history.
How many credit cards should I have?
Most people benefit most from 2–3 cards: a strong everyday cashback card, a travel rewards card, and optionally a no-fee backup. More than that usually adds complexity without meaningful extra rewards.
Does my credit card travel insurance replace standalone travel insurance?
Sometimes — but it depends heavily on the card, your age, trip cost, and whether you have pre-existing conditions. I review your card's policy document with you so you know exactly what's covered and what isn't before you travel.
What's the best card setup for newcomers to Canada?
Several Canadian banks offer secured or newcomer-specific cards that don't require a Canadian credit history. I can point you to the best options through FinlyWealth to start building credit from day one.
Can Avion points really book a Qatar QSuite?
Yes — and this is one of the best redemptions available to Canadians. You transfer RBC Avion points to British Airways Avios, then use Avios to book Qatar Airways flights (they're a BA partner). A Toronto–Doha QSuite Business Class ticket that retails for ~$7,500 CAD can be booked for ~35,000–55,000 Avios depending on availability. The 55,000-point welcome bonus on the RBC Avion Infinite can cover this entirely.
